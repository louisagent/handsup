# Handsup — Go-To-Market Plan
> Version 1.0 | March 2026

---

## The Core Problem: Chicken and Egg

Handsup is a content platform. Without content, there are no users. Without users, there's no content.

Every successful content platform solved this the same way: **seed one specific community with enough content to make the platform valuable, then expand.**

Reddit seeded subreddits manually. YouTube focused on specific video niches. Spotify launched with specific artists.

**Handsup's answer: seed one festival.** Get 500 clips from one event, make it undeniably useful for that crowd, then repeat.

---

## Phase 1 — Seed (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Get 500+ clips from 2–3 events in the catalogue. Make the search experience feel alive.

### Tactic 1: Festival Partner Deal (Priority #1)

Approach one Australian boutique festival — **Laneway Festival** is the first target.

**The pitch to them:**
> "We're launching a platform where your attendees can find and download footage from your event. We want Laneway to be our launch partner. We'll build you a branded event page, aggregate clip analytics, and feature you as the flagship festival. All free for your first year."

**What you get:**
- Access to their attendee email list (or a shoutout in their post-event email)
- One mention on their socials = thousands of highly engaged users who were just there
- Content seeding: they tell their crowd to upload to Handsup

**What they get:**
- Free platform, free publicity, free analytics on their event's content
- A new audience touchpoint post-event
- Positions them as innovative/community-oriented

**Target festivals for early outreach:**
1. Laneway Festival — boutique, Australian, community-driven
2. Meredith Music Festival — fiercely loyal crowd, "no dickheads" culture fits Handsup perfectly
3. Strawberry Fields — small, cult following, early adopter audience
4. Field Day — Sydney, New Years, extremely social-savvy crowd

### Tactic 2: The "Designated Filers" Program

For the first 3 events, recruit 10–20 "founding filmers" — people who will attend and agree to upload all their footage to Handsup.

Offer them: early access, a "Founding Member" badge, and free merch when it's ready.

Find them via: Reddit (/r/australianmusic, /r/laneway), festival Facebook groups, Discord servers.

### Tactic 3: Scrape + Redirect (Ethically)

Search YouTube and TikTok for existing concert footage from target festivals. Reach out to those uploaders:
> "Hey — you've got great footage from [event]. Would you upload it to Handsup? We're building the home for exactly this content and want your clips to be discoverable."

Many will say yes. It costs them nothing and gives them more exposure.

## Phase 2 — Growth (Months 3–9)

**Goal:** 10,000 registered users, 50+ events in the catalogue, 3 festival partnerships.

### Tactic 4: The Irony Marketing Strategy (TikTok/Instagram)

**The hook:** *"An app that helps you not use your phone at concerts — marketed on TikTok."*

This irony is the story. It's genuinely funny and shareable.

**Content formats:**
- "POV: You just found footage of yourself at Laneway because someone uploaded to Handsup"
- "The difference between watching the set through your phone vs actually being there [side by side]"
- Reaction videos: someone discovers a clip from a festival they went to
- "We interviewed 100 festival goers — here's what they actually regret" (filming instead of watching)

**Posting strategy:**
- 3x per week on TikTok and Reels
- Run through the festival season (Jan–Aug in Australia)
- Target hashtags: #festival #laneway #splendour #livemusic #phonefreeconcert

### Tactic 5: Artist + Creator Outreach

Target music journalists, festival bloggers, and micro-influencers in the Australian live music space.

**Pitch:** "You're at every festival. Handsup makes your footage discoverable and lets your followers download it directly. Here's early access."

Key targets:
- Music journalists at NME, Rolling Stone AU, Tone Deaf, TheMusic
- Festival review bloggers and Instagram pages (@lanewayfestival fans, etc.)
- Music photographers who already have large followings

### Tactic 6: Reddit + Discord Community Seeding

Be genuinely useful in communities, not spammy:

- Post in /r/australianmusic: "Looking for footage from Laneway 2025 — anyone have clips?"
- Build Handsup's own Discord for early members
- Answer threads about "how do I find footage from [show]" with genuine help + mention Handsup

### Tactic 7: Press

**Target publications for launch story:**

*Angle: "This Australian app wants to end phone culture at music festivals"*

- Tone Deaf (Australian music news)
- NME Australia
- The Music Network
- Broadsheet (lifestyle/culture, Melbourne/Sydney)
- Mediaweek (for the B2B festival angle)

One good press hit = thousands of organic downloads. Focus energy here before spending on paid.

## Phase 3 — Monetisation (Months 9–18)

**Goal:** First $10K MRR. Festival partnerships generating revenue.

### Revenue streams (in order of priority):

**1. Festival Partnership Packages — $2,000–$10,000/event**
- Branded event page + analytics dashboard
- Verified uploader access for their crew
- Post-event content report
- Featured placement in app

**2. Creator Rewards Programme**
- Top uploaders earn credits redeemable for festival tickets, merch, early access
- Funded by festival partners (they pay for the exposure to content creators)

**3. Premium Downloads (future)**
- Free: standard quality downloads
- Premium ($2.99/month): HD downloads, early access to new clips, no ads

**4. Festival Promotion**
- Festivals pay for featured placement in the Events tab
- Ticketing integration (affiliate commission on sales driven from Handsup)
