# Handsup — Competitor Research
> Researched March 2026

---

## Summary

**The short version: the gap is real and wide open.**

There is no dedicated platform for searching, browsing, and downloading crowd-sourced concert footage by artist/event/date. The closest attempts either pivoted away from consumers, never gained traction, or solve a completely different problem. Handsup has a clear first-mover opportunity in a market that is actively growing due to the phone-free concert trend.

---

## Direct Competitors (closest to Handsup's model)

### CrowdFlik
- **What it was:** App that let concertgoers sync multi-angle footage into one "flik"
- **Status:** ❌ Dead as a consumer app. Pivoted to B2B enterprise API/SDK in 2019. No longer available for end users.
- **Why it failed (likely):** Focused on the *creation* experience (syncing multiple angles), not the *discovery + download* experience. Too complex for casual users.
- **Handsup opportunity:** CrowdFlik proved the technical concept. Handsup executes the simpler, more intuitive version.

### FanVids.io
- **What it is:** Free app for artists to collect fan videos from shows
- **Focus:** Artist-centric (the artist collects footage, not fans discovering it)
- **Gap:** No public discovery, no search, no download for fans. It's a data tool for artists, not an experience for attendees.
- **Handsup opportunity:** Handsup is fan-first. The community owns the content library, not the artist's marketing team.

### FanFeed
- **What it is:** "Digital scrapbook" for live events — import photos from past shows
- **Focus:** Personal memory keeping, not community sharing or video
- **Gap:** No video, no community browsing, no search by artist/event
- **Handsup opportunity:** Different enough to not compete directly. Could actually complement.

### Crowd Clips
- **What it is:** Sports spectator content sharing
- **Focus:** Sports only
- **Gap:** Not music. Not festival-native. Different audience entirely.
- **Handsup opportunity:** Same model applied to a much larger, more digitally engaged audience.

## Indirect Competitors (where people currently go)

### YouTube
- **What people do:** Search "Tame Impala Laneway 2025" and hope someone uploaded it
- **Problems for users:**
  - No event-native structure (no artist → festival → date flow)
  - Quality is wildly inconsistent
  - Can't download easily
  - Algorithm buries obscure sets
  - Copyright claims remove content constantly
- **Handsup advantage:** Purpose-built for exactly this use case. Search that actually works.

### TikTok / Instagram Reels
- **What people do:** Post short clips, discover via hashtags
- **Problems:**
  - 60–90 second limit destroys full-set footage
  - Content is ephemeral (disappears from feeds fast)
  - No event-native discovery
  - No download without watermark
- **Handsup advantage:** Full clips, no time limit, permanent archive, clean downloads.

### Live Music Archive (archive.org)
- **What it is:** Legal archive of concert recordings, mostly older/jam band content
- **Focus:** Audio recordings, artist-approved only
- **Gap:** Not mobile-native, not video-first, not user-generated, extremely niche catalogue
- **Handsup advantage:** Modern app, video-first, any artist, any show.

## The Phone-Free Trend (Handsup's Tailwind)

This is the single biggest strategic insight from the research:

**The phone-free concert movement is accelerating hard.**

- By 2026, phone-free formats expected to shift from "novelty" to "expected" at immersive events, clubs, and residencies
- Artists using Yondr pouches: Jack White, Bob Dylan, Ghost (2025 world tour), Jill Scott (2026 tour)
- Yondr's own "Over Yondr Festival" proved multi-day phone-free events work
- Festival organisers actively looking for alternatives to phone-heavy crowds

**The problem Yondr creates for attendees:**
> "I can't film, but I still want the memory."

**Handsup is the answer to that problem.**

Positioning opportunity: **Handsup is the official companion to the phone-free concert movement.** When festivals enforce Yondr or "no filming" policies, Handsup is where the footage still lives — uploaded by the designated filmers, the crew, or the few attendees in unrestricted zones.

## Key Insight for the Pitch

The market has been tried before (CrowdFlik, FanVids) but never with:
1. **Fan-first discovery** — search for what *you* want to remember
2. **Clean download UX** — it's yours, no friction, no watermark
3. **Phone-free cultural alignment** — riding the Yondr/presence trend, not fighting it
4. **Festival partnership model** — B2B revenue alongside B2C growth

The window is open. No one has won this yet.
