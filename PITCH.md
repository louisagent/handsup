# 🙌 Handsup — Pitch One-Pager

> **Be there. We'll film it.**

---

## The Problem

At every music festival, thousands of people spend half the show looking at their phone screen instead of the stage. They film — badly — and their footage usually goes nowhere. Meanwhile, the people around them are annoyed, and nobody actually *experiences* the moment.

The irony? Everyone wants the footage after. They just don't want to be the one filming.

---

## The Solution

**Handsup** is a platform where festival goers can share and download video clips from events they attended — searchable by artist, location, and date.

Put your phone down. Someone else has the shot. You can grab it after the show.

---

## How It Works

1. **Attend** a festival or concert
2. **Search** on Handsup — find your set by artist, location, date
3. **Browse** clips uploaded by others who were there
4. **Download** directly to your camera roll — it's yours

If you *do* film something great, upload it. Tag it. The community benefits.

---

## Why Now

- Live music is back and bigger than ever post-COVID
- "Phone-free" concert culture is accelerating (artist-enforced Yondr pouches, audience frustration)
- There is no dedicated platform for crowd-sourced concert footage — YouTube and TikTok exist but have no event-native discovery
- Festivals are actively looking for ways to improve attendee experience

---

## Business Model

**Phase 1 — Free**
- Free app, free uploads, free downloads
- Build the content library and user base

**Phase 2 — Creator Incentives**
- Top uploaders earn rewards (credits, merch, festival tickets)
- Festivals partner to have official crew upload under their brand
- Premium events pay to have curated coverage

**Phase 3 — Platform Revenue**
- Festival partnerships — promoted event pages, ticketing integrations
- Exclusive content packages (e.g. full-set HD downloads for $2.99)
- B2B: festivals pay for crowd coverage as a service

---

## Target Users

**Primary:** 18–35 festival attendees who want the memory without the effort
**Secondary:** The minority who *do* film — they get recognition and rewards
**B2B:** Festival organisers who want engagement data and content without hiring full crews

---

## Traction Potential

- Laneway, Splendour, Glastonbury, Coachella — combined attendance exceeds 350,000 annually
- Each of those events has an intensely loyal repeat audience with high social sharing rates
- One good festival partner = seeding the platform with thousands of clips overnight
